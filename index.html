I have been playing cello for nine years, through that time I have played for various events: award ceremonies, fashion shows, community events. This is a path I have curated through practicing my "talent" and putting dedication to the art. My message has changed as I learn more about Native American history, my Dine culture, and myself introspectively. I am intentional when I wear my traditional regalia and when I play the contemporary art form of cello. This is to show the resilience and emmpathy I have gained throughout the years, that I have put myself in between both worlds of western and indigenous culture, to share something beautiful. Before colonization, to the early 19th century and too now i am practicing art forms that are generations far from their origin. My gift is to present them to an audience. Listening to live music has us ground ourselves with our current position in our lives, where we make meaning of art. It's resilient, so I play resilient, as an indigenous cellist.
